A practical tool for beginner contact centre agents — designed to keep things simple.
What this is

This CRM simulator recreates the core experience of a real contact centre system — customer lookup, case logging, call timing, and wrap-up — without the noise of a live production environment. It's built for practice, onboarding, and building confidence before you touch a real customer record.

Everything you type is saved automatically to your browser. Nothing leaves your device — there's no database, no server, no risk of affecting real customers.

Design philosophy
Start simple, add complexity as you grow. The Settings panel (left edge of screen) lets you hide any panel you don't need yet. As a beginner, you don't need to see everything at once — turn off the panels that distract you and focus on just one or two things at a time.

Real contact centre screens are dense and overwhelming on day one. This tool is designed so you can gradually expose yourself to more of the screen as you build familiarity — rather than trying to understand everything simultaneously.

Suggested beginner workflow
  1. 1Pick a company from the tabs at the top — each one has its own colour theme and queue setup, just like switching between clients.
  2. 2Hit ▶ START on the call timer (top right) the moment a call begins. This builds the habit of tracking handle time from day one.
  3. 3Fill in Caller Identity first — name, account number, phone. Verifying the caller's identity is always step one on a real call.
  4. 4Move to the Case / Ticket panel in the centre. Select a category, set the status, and write case notes as the call progresses — don't wait until the end.
  5. 5Use Quick Actions on the right to simulate what you'd do — transfer, hold, escalate. Click them during your practice scenario to build muscle memory.
  6. 6Stop the timer, fill in the Wrap-Up Bar at the bottom with a disposition code, then hit Save & Close. This is After Call Work (ACW) — every second counts.
Sticky notes

Use the ✎ + NOTE button (bottom right) to create floating notes during a call — great for jotting down order numbers, phonetic spellings, or things to double-check. Notes are draggable and persist between sessions. Each new note starts with a customer detail template.

Tips for practice
Less is more at first. In Settings, try turning off Account Status, Flags, and History. Practice the core loop: identity → case notes → wrap-up. Add panels back one by one each session.
Treat every field as real. Type realistic fake data — made-up names, plausible order numbers, actual issue descriptions. The more seriously you treat the simulation, the more transferable the skills.
You can reopen this guide any time from the ? HELP button in the Settings panel.
